Diving Deeper into my summer

Over the summer I was determined to learn as many new things as I could. Two things in particular were golf and tennis. As I am getting older I figured I need to start learning sports that require less physical strain on my body. Golf is largely considered the best sport for this. I began going to the driving range around 4 times a week this summer and eventually got good enought to go out on the course. Now my driver is still not great, but my short game is pretty decent. I also began playing tennis a lot. My coworker played tennis in high school and gave me a lesson after we were watching the wimbledon during our shift. My goal was to get good enough to play casually with friends and family, and I have definitely accomplished this. I recruited one of my good friends to learn with me and we improved together over the summer. We are now able to rally consistently and play full sets.

Tips I suggest for golf and tennis

  • Golf: focus on accuracy over power. Power will come over time.
  • Tennis: consistency in your stance and footwork is key. Power is generated from your lower body up.
  • Both: Practice makes perfect. You will get better over time if you practice consistently.
